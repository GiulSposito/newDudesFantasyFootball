## Matchups Recap

### Paulinia Robots Waxes Campinas Giants, Climbs to 8th Place 

Paulinia Robots picked up 27.50 points from Tyreek Hill (83 Rec Yds, 2 TD) and 22.70 from D'Ernest Johnson (99 Rsh Yds, 58 Rec Yds) to bulldoze Campinas Giants 127.92 to 81.32. This beatdown was revenge for a loss to Campinas Giants in their last meeting a season ago. These two teams are tied for eighth place with 5-5 records, but Paulinia Robots has a clear lead in points, 1,231.58 to 1,063.46. To make matters worse, Campinas Giants received no points from Mike Gesicki.

 There was one noteworthy starter on Paulinia Robots that let some chances slip through his hands. Despite being targeted eight times, Tyler Lockett could only manage two catches.

 While Nyheim Hines led Paulinia Robots weeks (with 20.80 points), Hill has now been the team's leading scorer in two of the last three matches.

 In Week 11, Paulinia Robots will face top-ranked Limeira Dead Rabbits (8-2, 1,144.28). Campinas Giants takes on Barao Butchers (5-5, 1,134.70).

### Sorocaba Wild Mules Picks Up Victory over London Knights for 4th in a Row 

Rhamondre Stevenson picked up 27.40 points, an upgrade on his projection of 6.85, as Sorocaba Wild Mules defeated London Knights 111.10 to 82.52. London Knights can't find any answers, losing their last four matchups. On the other hand, Sorocaba Wild Mules is headed in the opposite direction after winning their fourth straight. Jonathan Taylor led London Knights with 24.60 points (116 Rsh Yds, 1 TD) while Mark Andrews brought in 18.30 (63 Rec Yds, 1 TD). Sorocaba Wild Mules started one player with zero points while London Knights served up two goose eggs, meaning both teams could have scored more points. Sorocaba Wild Mules (6-4, 1,212.34 points) climbs into fourth place and London Knights (2-8, 1,175.66 points) stays in 13th place.

 Since Stevenson was the leading point-getter for Sorocaba Wild Mules, it means a different player has now topped the team in each of the past five weeks. With 21.00 points, Aaron Jones paced the squad in Week 6, while Joe Burrow put up 26.64 points to lead the way in Week 7. Chris Godwin (28.00 points) was tops in Week 8 and Justin Herbert (31.64 points) led last week.

 Sorocaba Wild Mules gets a challenge next week when they go up against Indaiatuba Blues (6-4, 1,273.16), who averages a league-leading 127.32 points per game. London Knights plays Amparo Bikers (6-4, 1,160.70).

### Indaiatuba Riders Comes Back to Wax Rio Claro Farmers, Moves Up to 2nd Place 

Indaiatuba Riders got the highest score by a player this week from Patrick Mahomes with 36.24 points (406 Pas Yds, 5 TD) and erased a 5.78-point deficit on Sunday night, thumping Rio Claro Farmers 115.04 to 75.18. This was a back-and-forth battle that saw the lead change hands three times, with the largest lead being 39.86 points after Monday night. This beatdown was revenge for a loss to Rio Claro Farmers in their last meeting a season ago. With Antonio Brown delivering zero points for Indaiatuba Riders, the margin could have been bigger. Indaiatuba Riders (7-3, 1,148.30 points) climbs into second place and Rio Claro Farmers (5-5, 1,177.48 points) drops to ninth place.

 Jacobs had plenty of opportunities for Rio Claro Farmers, but could only manage 36 total yards on 12 touches.

 While Marquise Brown led Indaiatuba Riders weeks (with 20.60 points), Mahomes has now been the team's leading scorer in two of the past three matches.

 Indaiatuba Riders will match up against Limeira Jetsons (7-3, 1,071.08) next week. Rio Claro Farmers takes on Sorocaba Steelers (1-9, 942.52), who sits in last place.

### Powered by Stars, Amparo Bikers Blows Up Sorocaba Steelers 

With the trio of Deebo Samuel, A.J. Dillon, and the Dallas Cowboys DEF leading the way, Amparo Bikers humiliated Sorocaba Steelers 138.60 to 57.58. Combining for 78.10 points, that threesome alone surpassed the total of Sorocaba Steelers. Samuel scored two touchdowns, accounting for 30.30 points (97 Rec Yds, 36 Rsh Yds, 1 Rec TD, 1 Rsh TD). Dillon delivered two touchdowns to contribute 26.80 points (66 Rsh Yds, 62 Rec Yds, 2 TD), while the Dallas D helped out with 21.00 points (3 PA, 1 TD, 2 Sack, 3 Int). Samuel also recorded the second-highest point total in the league this week, with Dillon tallying his highest point total of the season. The 81.02-point win for Amparo Bikers was the second-largest in the league this year. Amparo Bikers (6-4, 1,160.70 points) falls to sixth place, while Sorocaba Steelers (1-9, 942.52 points) is still looking up at everyone from the cellar.

 Cordarrelle Patterson really deserved more touches for Sorocaba Steelers, as he put up a whopping 39 total yards on just five touches.

 While Josh Allen led Amparo Bikers in Week 8 (with 29.46 points) and Darren Waller last week (16.20 points), Samuel has now been the team's top point-getter twice in the last four weeks.

 Amparo Bikers takes on London Knights (2-8, 1,175.66) in Week 11, while Sorocaba Steelers goes up against Rio Claro Farmers (5-5, 1,177.48).

### Indaiatuba Pats Tops Limeira Jetsons, Ousts Them from 1st Place 

Indaiatuba Pats (seventh place, 5-5, 1,106.28 points) dumps Limeira Jetsons (7-3, 1,071.08 points) out of the top spot in the league after beating them 111.80 to 89.88. Justin Jefferson (143 Rec Yds) led Indaiatuba Pats with 22.90 points while Travis Kelce (119 Rec Yds) scored 19.90. Indaiatuba Pats took a 5.80-point lead on Sunday afternoon and maintained that advantage for the rest of the matchup. Indaiatuba Pats put forth their best effort and put up 27.4% more than their projected 87.74 points, while Limeira Jetsons failed to meet expectations and scored only 88.4% of their projected 101.67. With Jason Myers delivering zero points for Indaiatuba Pats, the margin could have been larger.

 While Jimmy Garoppolo led Indaiatuba Pats with 19.34 points last week, Jefferson paced the squad this week with 22.90.

 Indaiatuba Pats squares off against Goncalves Miners (2-8, 1,002.16) next week, while Limeira Jetsons goes up against Indaiatuba Riders (7-3, 1,148.30).

### Behind Three Big Performances, Indaiatuba Blues Brings Down Barao Butchers 

Carried by the triumvirate of Darrel Williams, Dak Prescott, and Cooper Kupp, Indaiatuba Blues picked up a win against Barao Butchers 147.84 to 105.24. That threesome joined forces to tally 78.94 points, significantly more than Derek Carr, Hunter Renfrow, and Najee Harris (53.14 combined points), the top three on Barao Butchers. Williams (43 Rsh Yds, 101 Rec Yds, 1 Rec TD) delivered 29.40 points, with Prescott (296 Pas Yds, 2 Pas TD, 1 Rsh TD) and Kupp (122 Rec Yds) chipping in 26.34 and 23.20 points, respectively. Also, Williams recorded his highest point total of the season. With 147.84 points, Indaiatuba Blues had the highest total in the league this week. Indaiatuba Blues (6-4, 1,273.16 points) climbs into fifth place, while Barao Butchers (5-5, 1,134.70 points) drops to 10th place.

 While Cooper Kupp led Indaiatuba Blues with 20.50 points in Week 9, Williams paced the squad this week with 29.40.

 Indaiatuba Blues plays Sorocaba Wild Mules (6-4, 1,212.34) next week, while Barao Butchers goes up against Campinas Giants (5-5, 1,063.46).

 While Damien Harris led Limeira Dead Rabbits in Week 7 (with 25.30 points) and CeeDee Lamb in Week 8 (17.20 points), Lamb has now been the team's leading scorer in three of the last five weeks.
