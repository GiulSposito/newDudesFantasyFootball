## Matchups Recap

### Paulinia Robots Rocks Indaiatuba Pats for 14th Straight Win 

In a shootout between this week's first and third-highest-scoring teams, Paulinia Robots crushed Indaiatuba Pats 168.54 to 112.26. Indaiatuba Pats falls to 12-2 (2nd place) and breaks a six-game winning streak. Paulinia Robots (14-0) is in first place and has the highest points per game average at 134.13. Indaiatuba Pats actually finished with more total yards (812 to 756), despite not getting the win. Josh Allen (251 Pas Yds, 78 Rsh Yds, 1 2PT, 3 Pas TD, 1 Rsh TD) accumulated the highest score of the week (37.84) and Jahmyr Gibbs scored 37.00 points to lead Paulinia Robots. DK Metcalf (148 Rec Yds) recorded 21.80 points to score 83.8% more than projected (11.86) for Indaiatuba Pats. This is the second tough loss this season for Indaiatuba Pats. Their last one was to BroncosLoucura in Week 7, 138.94 to 120.44.

 Keaton Mitchell really deserved more touches for Indaiatuba Pats, as he put up a whopping 76 total yards on just six touches.

 While Jahmyr Gibbs led Paulinia Robots in Week 12 (with 55.40 points) and Rashee Rice last week (29.40 points), Allen has now been the team's high point-getter twice in the last four matches. It wasn't all bad news for Indaiatuba Pats, as they did put together a team of real-life winners. Every starter except Keaton Mitchell played on a winning NFL team in Week 14.

 Both Paulinia Robots and Indaiatuba Pats made the playoffs, which begin next week.

### Indaiatuba Riders Wins Comeback Nailbiter over Niners BH, Moves Up to 13th Place 

Indaiatuba Riders had an excellent primetime showing with 21.62 points on Sunday Night Football and 15.00 on Monday (a combined 40% of their total score) to sneak past Niners BH 90.92 to 90.70. Indaiatuba Riders was led by Bucky Irving, who contributed 16.10 points in the tight matchup (55 Rsh Yds, 26 Rec Yds, 1 Rec TD). The 0.22-point win represented the second-smallest in the league this year, as well. Indaiatuba Riders (4-10, 1,264.08 points) climbs into 13th place, while Niners BH (3-11, 1,293.00 points) drops to 14th place.

 There were a few players in this matchup that had plenty of chances but left some points out on the field. On the winning side, Kareem Hunt and Kenneth Walker III got 23 total touches but only tallied 59 all-purpose yards between them. For Niners BH, Ashton Jeanty managed just 38 yards on 12 touches.

 While Bryce Young led Indaiatuba Riders with 22.54 points last week, Irving paced the squad this week with 16.10.

 Indaiatuba Riders takes on Sorocaba Steelers (5-9, 1,390.48) next week, while Niners BH faces London Knights (6-8, 1,426.52).

### Sorocaba Steelers Rips Apart Holambra Dutch 

The combo of Harold Fannin Jr. and Christian Watson led the charge, as Sorocaba Steelers tore apart Holambra Dutch 138.46 to 56.14. That tandem combined to tally 50.30 points, topping the production from RJ Harvey and Dak Prescott (40.44 combined points), the top two on Holambra Dutch. Watson scored two touchdowns, accounting for 24.90 points (89 Rec Yds, 2 TD). For the winners, Jr. also helped out with 25.40 points (114 Rec Yds, 1 TD). This beatdown was revenge for a loss to Holambra Dutch in their last meeting a season ago. To make matters worse, Holambra Dutch had a starter score zero points (Tyrone Tracy Jr.). Sorocaba Steelers (5-9, 1,390.48 points) remains in 12th place and Holambra Dutch (2-12, 1,094.70 points) stays in 15th place.

 Since Fannin was the leading scorer for Sorocaba Steelers, it means a different player has now led the team in each of the past seven matches.

With the win, Limeira Jetsons secures a playoff spot. This close win was revenge for a loss to London Knights in their last meeting a season ago. Kenneth Gainwell (acquired via free agency in Week 1) made a big impact, posting 16.20 points. The result might have been even worse as Limeira Jetsons won despite two starters scoring zero points. Limeira Jetsons (8-6, 1,457.76 points) falls to eighth place and London Knights (6-8, 1,426.52 points) drops to 11th place.

 London Knights had one important starter that missed out on some chances. Despite being targeted nine times, Emeka Egbuka could only manage two catches.

 Since Nacua was the leading point-getter for Limeira Jetsons, it means a different player has now topped the team in each of the past five weeks. With 22.40 points, Kyren Williams paced the squad in Week 10, while Tetairoa McMillan put up 33.00 points to lead the way in Week 11. Kenneth Gainwell (18.20 points) was tops in Week 12 and Trevor Lawrence (21.66 points) led in Week 13.
