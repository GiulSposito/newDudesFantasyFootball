## Matchups Recap

### Powered by a Signature Performance from Rashee Rice, Paulinia Robots Tops Barao Butchers 

With the contribution from Rashee Rice paving the way, Paulinia Robots got a victory over Barao Butchers 91.22 to 68.44. In the 22.78-point victory for Paulinia Robots, Rice went off for 29.40 points. He scored two touchdowns as part of his big outing (92 Rec Yds, 2 TD). He also recorded his highest point total of the season. On top of the loss, Barao Butchers had a starter score zero points (Calvin Austin III). Paulinia Robots (13-0, 1,709.26 points) remains in first place, while Barao Butchers (1-12, 1,107.16 points) stays mired in last place.

 Isiah Pacheco really deserved more touches for Barao Butchers, as he put up a whopping 33 total yards on just five touches.

 While Jahmyr Gibbs led Paulinia Robots with 55.40 points in Week 12, Rice paced the squad this week with 29.40.

 In Week 14, Paulinia Robots battles Indaiatuba Pats (12-1, 1,603.52), who puts up a solid 123.35 points per game. Barao Butchers gets BroncosLoucura (6-7, 1,452.54).

### Sorocaba Wild Mules Beats Campinas Giants, Streak Now at Nine 

Sorocaba Wild Mules got 20.80 points from D'Andre Swift (125 Rsh Yds, 1 TD) and 19.40 from De'Von Achane (134 Rsh Yds, 1 TD), taking down Campinas Giants 116.74 to 88.22. Sorocaba Wild Mules took an 18.00-point lead on Thursday and remained in front for the rest of the matchup. This win was revenge for a loss to Campinas Giants in their last meeting a season ago. Daniel Jones (acquired via free agency in Week 1) made a big impact, posting 16.14 points. Sorocaba Wild Mules (10-3, 1,476.38 points) remains in third place and Campinas Giants (8-5, 1,376.12 points) stays in sixth place.

 Campinas Giants had one glaring starter that missed out on some chances. Despite being targeted seven times, Zay Flowers could only manage two catches.

 While George Pickens led Sorocaba Wild Mules last week with 29.60 points, Swift paved the way this week with 20.80. Also, with 35 touches this week for James Cook, it means that his touches have now increased in three consecutive games.

 Next week, Sorocaba Wild Mules will face Limeira Dead Rabbits (8-5, 1,480.58). Campinas Giants plays Amparo Bikers (7-6, 1,523.88), who is averaging a stout 117.22 points per game.

Indaiatuba Riders (3-10, 1,173.16 points) remains in 14th place and Holambra Dutch (2-11, 1,038.56 points) stays in 15th place.

 Tracy had plenty of opportunities for Holambra Dutch, but could only manage 36 total yards on 11 touches.

### London Knights Beats Rio Claro Farmers to Capture 10th Place 

In a shootout between this week's first and third-highest-scoring teams, London Knights defeated Rio Claro Farmers 140.96 to 131.40. This is the most a team has scored this season without getting a victory. The loss drops Rio Claro Farmers to 8-5, but they maintain their fourth-place position in the standings. Rio Claro Farmers still clinched it's spot in the postseason this week. The scoring output from London Knights was unexpected given that they were only expected to score 99.75 points. Rio Claro Farmers scored 124.7% of their projected 105.34. A.J. Brown (132 Rec Yds, 2 TD) amassed the highest score this week (35.20) and Jameson Williams scored 26.90 points to lead London Knights. Bijan Robinson (142 Rsh Yds, 51 Rec Yds, 1 TD) put up the second-highest point total of the week (30.30) and Terry McLaurin scored 22.60 points for Rio Claro Farmers.

 Breaking down the outcome, Rio Claro Farmers can point the finger at their WR, Michael Pittman. Pittman was projected to score 12.17 points, actually produced 2.30, and that difference was enough for London Knights to take the win.

 London Knights was on another level with their wideouts, as they started two of the league's top-10 overall WRs this week (including Brown, who was best overall). Brown has now led London Knights in scoring in back-to-back weeks.

 London Knights takes on Limeira Jetsons (7-6, 1,359.50) in Week 14, while Rio Claro Farmers gets Indaiatuba Blues (6-7, 1,296.64).

### Indaiatuba Pats Gets Win over Limeira Dead Rabbits for 6th Straight Win 

The combo of Davante Adams and Jakobi Meyers led the charge, as Indaiatuba Pats defeated Limeira Dead Rabbits 125.34 to 101.84. That tandem teamed up to register 43.10 points, clearly outperforming Kyle Monangai and Travis Kelce (34.50 combined points), the top two on Limeira Dead Rabbits. Adams scored two touchdowns, adding 21.80 points (58 Rec Yds, 2 TD). Meyers also chipped in 21.30 points (90 Rec Yds, 1 TD) for the winners. Limeira Dead Rabbits is not on the same level as Indaiatuba Pats, who extended their career unbeaten streak to three games in the series. Adding insult to injury, Limeira Dead Rabbits had a starter score zero points (Amon-Ra St. Brown). Indaiatuba Pats (12-1, 1,603.52 points) remains in second place and Limeira Dead Rabbits (8-5, 1,480.58 points) stays in fifth place.

 Christian McCaffrey had plenty of chances for Indaiatuba Pats, but he logged just 74 total yards on 24 touches.

 While Christian McCaffrey led Indaiatuba Pats with 27.20 points in Week 12, Adams paced the squad this week with 21.80.

 Indaiatuba Pats will match up against top-ranked Paulinia Robots (13-0, 1,709.26) next week. Limeira Dead Rabbits plays Sorocaba Wild Mules (10-3, 1,476.38).
