## Matchups Recap

### Three Is the Magic Number, as Paulinia Robots Pounds Limeira Dead Rabbits 

With the trio of Jahmyr Gibbs, Jaxon Smith-Njigba, and Rashee Rice leading the way, Paulinia Robots humiliated Limeira Dead Rabbits 186.12 to 94.90. That threesome combined to score 116.60 points, surpassing the total of Limeira Dead Rabbits. Gibbs rushed for 219 yards, accounting for 55.40 points (219 Rsh Yds, 45 Rec Yds, 2 Rsh TD, 1 Rec TD). Smith-Njigba delivered 167 receiving yards to contribute 37.10 points (167 Rec Yds, 2 TD), while Rice chipped in 24.10 points (141 Rec Yds). Gibbs also recorded the highest point total in the league this season, with Smith-Njigba tallying the second-highest point total in the league this week. Paulinia Robots had the highest score in the league this year, as well as the largest margin of victory. Limeira Dead Rabbits (8-4, 1,378.74 points) drops to fifth place, while Paulinia Robots (12-0, 1,618.04 points) remains in first place.

 There was one starter on Limeira Dead Rabbits that left points out on the field. Despite being targeted nine times, Rome Odunze could manage just three catches.

 The leading scorer for Paulinia Robots has flip-flopped the last few weeks. Josh Allen led the team with 28.82 points in Week 9 and 42.68 points matches, while Gibbs led the way in Week 10 (with 38.20 points) and then again this week.

 Next week, Paulinia Robots matches up against the last-place team in the league, Barao Butchers (1-11, 1,038.72). Limeira Dead Rabbits plays Indaiatuba Pats (11-1, 1,478.18), who is averaging a stout 123.18 points per game.

### Limeira Jetsons Rallies Late to Beat Indaiatuba Riders, Notches 5th in a Row 

Limeira Jetsons got 18.20 points from Kenneth Gainwell (92 Rsh Yds, 30 Rec Yds) and 17.14 from Trevor Lawrence (256 Pas Yds, 29 Rsh Yds, 3 TD/3 Int), taking down Indaiatuba Riders 103.34 to 93.26. Limeira Jetsons came into the Sunday night game facing a 17.86-point deficit, but scored 27.70 for the comeback victory. This matchup included three lead changes, with the largest lead being 33.90 points after Sunday afternoon. Limeira Jetsons is a tough opponent for anyone after winning their last five matchups. On top of the loss, Indaiatuba Riders had a starter score zero points (Bucky Irving). Limeira Jetsons (7-5, 1,258.24 points) remains in seventh place and Indaiatuba Riders (2-10, 1,075.12 points) stays in 14th place.

 Each squad had a fairly important starter that missed out on some chances. For Limeira Jetsons, Tetairoa McMillan saw a total of seven targets, but was only able to reel in two catches. Tre Tucker put on a similar showing for Indaiatuba Riders, as he was only able to snag three passes on 10 targets.

 Since Gainwell was the high scorer for Limeira Jetsons, it means that a different player has now led the team in each of the last six weeks. With 17.64 points, Trevor Lawrence paced the squad in Week 7, while Troy Franklin (26.90 points) was team-leader in Week 8 and Puka Nacua (22.80 points) led the way in Week 9. Kyren Williams (22.40 points) was tops in Week 10 and Tetairoa McMillan (33.00 points) led in Week 11.

 Next week, Limeira Jetsons squares off against Sorocaba Steelers (3-9, 1,138.98), while Indaiatuba Riders matches up with Holambra Dutch (2-10, 947.96).
